Baitul Haque Jame Masjid

A place for salah, learning, and community

Our masjid is located in Kayestogram (Kushumbag), Hetimgonj, Golapganj, Sylhet, and was established on 26 July 2024 to serve as a house of Allah for the local Muslim community. It is a place where worshippers come together for prayer, remembrance of Allah, and spiritual growth in an atmosphere of peace, faith, and unity.
More than a place of worship, the masjid is a spiritual home that brings families and neighbours together, strengthens the bonds of brotherhood, and inspires a deeper connection with Islam and the community.

The Five Pillars of Islam

The foundational core of a Muslim's life and faith, guiding our devotion to Allah and our duty to uplift the community through charity and service.

Iman (Faith)

The declaration of faith: “There is no God but Allah, and Muhammad (S:) is His messenger,” signifying total devotion.

  • Absolute Monotheism
  • Intentionality (Niyyah)

Salah (Prayer)

The five daily prayers performed at dawn, noon, afternoon, sunset, and night, connecting the believer to the Divine.

  • Fajr
  • Dhuhr
  • Asr
  • Maghrib
  • Isha

Sawm (Fasting)

Abstaining from food, drink, and worldly desires from dawn until sunset during the holy month of Ramadan.

“Whoever fasts with faith and seeking reward, his previous sins will be forgiven.”
(- Sahih al-Bukhari)

Zakat (Almsgiving)

The mandatory charitable contribution of 2.5% of one’s wealth to help the poor and needy, purifying one’s soul and finances.

2.5%

Wealth Ratio

Hajj (Pilgrimage)

The pilgrimage to the holy city of Mecca, a duty for every Muslim who is physically and financially able at least once in their lifetime.
